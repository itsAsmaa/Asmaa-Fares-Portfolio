About Me

Engineer by Design,
Researcher by Drive

I'm a fifth-year Computer Systems Engineering student at Birzeit University — one of the strongest technical programs in Palestine. My work sits at a rare intersection: I design neural networks and write UVM testbenches.

My graduation thesis on AI Speech-Based Emotion Recognition compared traditional ML ensembles against CNN-BiLSTM deep learning on RAVDESS, and proposed a novel arousal-based clustering strategy. It was published at ICCRTEE 2025.

On the AI/ML side I've fine-tuned CLIP for text-to-video retrieval, built multimodal fusion pipelines, and achieved 99.50% accuracy on handwritten Arabic numeral recognition using EfficientNetB3.

On the hardware side I interned at Orion VLSI Technologies for 240 hours, verified cryptographic hardware in SystemVerilog/UVM, and built a 5-stage pipelined RISC processor from scratch.

I teach, mentor, and document obsessively. Every project has a report, every session a recording.

Research

Published Work

📄 ICCRTEE 2025
AI Speech-Based Emotion Recognition using Traditional ML and CNN-BiLSTM with Arousal-Based Clustering
Asmaa Fares & Noor Mouadi — Supervised by Dr. Jamal Siam · Birzeit University, 2025
Compares ensemble traditional ML (SVM + Random Forest soft voting on 100+ acoustic features) against CNN-BiLSTM deep learning for speech emotion recognition on RAVDESS. Proposes a novel arousal-based clustering framework partitioning emotions into high-arousal (angry, fearful, happy, surprised) and low-arousal (calm, disgust, neutral, sad) clusters, and evaluates a hierarchical two-stage recognition system. The traditional ML baseline achieves 89.49% on 5 emotions and 83.68% on 8 emotions, significantly outperforming the flat deep learning baseline of 56%.
