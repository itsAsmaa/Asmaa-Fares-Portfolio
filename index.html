Research

Published Work

ICCRTEE 2025 — International Conference
AI Speech-Based Emotion Recognition using Traditional ML and CNN-BiLSTM with Arousal-Based Clustering
Asmaa Fares, Noor Mouadi — Supervised by Dr. Jamal Siam · Birzeit University, 2025
Compares ensemble traditional ML (SVM + Random Forest soft voting) against CNN-BiLSTM deep learning for speech emotion recognition on RAVDESS. Proposes a novel arousal-based clustering framework — partitioning emotions into high-arousal (angry, fearful, happy, surprised) and low-arousal (calm, disgust, neutral, sad) clusters — and evaluates a hierarchical two-stage recognition system. The traditional ML baseline achieves 89.49% on 5 emotions and 83.68% on 8 emotions, significantly outperforming the flat deep learning baseline of 56%.
